I am a third-year undergraduate at Beijing Jiaotong University, majoring in Data Science (expected graduation June 2027). My research interest lies at the intersection of operating systems, distributed systems, and AI infrastructure.

Specifically, I am interested in how disaggregated hardware architectures — where CPU, memory, and storage become independent network-attached components — can be leveraged to improve resource utilization and reduce inference latency in large-scale LLM serving systems.

I have hands-on experience with RDMA-based communication, distributed consensus (Raft), OS kernel development (RISC-V / ByteOS), and LSM-Tree storage engines. I am currently exploring GPU memory management in the context of KV Cache optimization for LLM inference.

I am actively seeking research internship opportunities and PhD positions for Fall 2028. I was introduced to this research area through reading LegoOS (OSDI '18) and related work on hardware resource disaggregation.

Research Interests

研究方向

01 · HARDWARE

Hardware Disaggregation

Separating CPU, memory, and storage into independent network-attached components. How should OS abstractions be redesigned when hardware is no longer co-located? (LegoOS, Splitkernel)

02 · AI SYSTEMS

LLM Inference Systems

Disaggregated serving architectures that separate Prefill and Decode stages across machines. KV Cache memory management, cross-node RDMA transfer, and scheduling under SLO constraints. (DistServe, Splitwise, Mooncake)

03 · OS

OS for AI Workloads

How should OS-level scheduling, memory management, and I/O subsystems be redesigned to better support AI inference? Bridging classical systems research and modern AI infrastructure.

04 · STORAGE

Distributed Storage

Consistency, fault tolerance, and I/O performance tradeoffs in distributed KV stores. Relevant to persistent KV Cache and checkpoint storage in large-scale training and inference pipelines.

Complete distributed KV store: Raft consensus in Go with Jepsen fault-injection validation for linearizability, plus C++ LSM-Tree engine using O_DIRECT write path and mmap read path. Multi-tenant I/O isolation via cgroups v2. Write amplification ≤10×.
